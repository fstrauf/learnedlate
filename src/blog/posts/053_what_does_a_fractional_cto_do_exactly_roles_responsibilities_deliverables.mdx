# What Does a Fractional CTO Do? Roles & Deliverables

What Does a Fractional CTO Do? Roles & Deliverables — a fractional CTO provides strategic technology leadership on a part-time basis, handling technical roadmaps, team building, and engineering operations without the cost of a full-time executive. They bridge the gap between your business goals and technical execution.

A fractional CTO is a part-time technology executive who provides strategic leadership, oversees engineering teams, and drives digital transformation without the cost of a full-time hire. They typically manage technical roadmaps, evaluate vendor partnerships, and ensure your infrastructure scales with business growth.

Not vague consultant-speak. Not buzzwords. You want to know what actually shows up in your calendar, what decisions they'll own, and what deliverables you'll receive for your investment.

This guide breaks down the specific roles, responsibilities, and outputs of a fractional CTO—so you know exactly what to expect.

## What Is a Fractional CTO, Really?

A fractional CTO is an experienced technology executive who works with your company on a part-time basis—typically 1-3 days per week. They provide the strategic leadership of a full-time CTO without the full-time cost or commitment.

Unlike a consultant who delivers a report and leaves, a fractional CTO becomes an embedded member of your leadership team. They attend your meetings, know your product, understand your customers, and stick around to see decisions through.

## Core Responsibilities: What They Actually Do

A fractional CTO's work typically falls into four key areas. Here's what each looks like in practice.

### 1. Strategic Technical Leadership

This is the highest-value work a fractional CTO delivers. They're responsible for ensuring your technology serves your business goals—not the other way around.

**What this looks like day-to-day:**

- **Technology roadmap development**: Creating a 6-18 month plan that aligns technical work with business priorities
- **Architecture decision-making**: Choosing between build vs. buy, evaluating frameworks, planning infrastructure
- **Technical risk assessment**: Identifying security vulnerabilities, scalability bottlenecks, and technical debt before they become crises
- **Investment preparation**: Getting your codebase, documentation, and team ready for due diligence

**Typical deliverables:**
- Technical roadmap document (updated quarterly)
- Architecture decision records (ADRs) for major choices
- Risk assessment reports
- Board presentation materials on technical strategy

### 2. Team Building & Leadership

Most founders hire a fractional CTO because they need someone to manage technical people—whether that's a single contractor or a growing engineering team.

**What this looks like day-to-day:**

- **Hiring strategy**: Defining roles, creating interview processes, evaluating candidates
- **Team structure**: Organising engineers into effective teams as you scale
- **Performance management**: Setting expectations, conducting reviews, addressing underperformance
- **Culture development**: Establishing how your engineering team works together
- **Mentorship**: Leveling up junior developers and growing technical leaders

**Typical deliverables:**
- Job descriptions and hiring rubrics
- Interview processes and technical assessments
- Team structure diagrams and role definitions
- Career ladder documentation
- Regular one-on-one meetings with team members

### 3. Operational Excellence

Beyond strategy and people, fractional CTOs establish the processes that keep engineering teams productive and products reliable.

**What this looks like day-to-day:**

- **Development process design**: Setting up workflows for how code gets written, reviewed, and deployed
- **Quality assurance**: Implementing testing strategies, code review practices, and release processes
- **Security and compliance**: Ensuring your systems meet required standards (SOC 2, GDPR, etc.)
- **Vendor management**: Evaluating and negotiating with cloud providers, SaaS tools, and development agencies
- **Budget oversight**: Planning and monitoring technology spend

**Typical deliverables:**
- Development workflow documentation
- Security audit reports and remediation plans
- Vendor evaluation matrices
- Monthly technology budget reviews
- Incident response procedures

### 4. Translation Between Business and Technology

Perhaps the most underrated role of a fractional CTO is bridging the communication gap between technical and non-technical stakeholders.

**What this looks like day-to-day:**

- **Stakeholder communication**: Explaining technical concepts to investors, board members, and non-technical founders
- **Estimation and planning**: Translating business timelines into realistic technical delivery schedules
- **Trade-off analysis**: Helping the business understand the cost and implications of technical decisions
- **Requirements clarification**: Ensuring engineers understand what the business actually needs

**Typical deliverables:**
- Executive summaries of technical topics
- Effort estimation for product initiatives
- Decision memos outlining technical trade-offs
- Board-ready presentations on technical matters

## What a Fractional CTO Does NOT Do

Setting clear expectations means understanding the boundaries too. Here's what falls outside a typical fractional CTO scope:

### Not a Full-Time Engineer

Fractional CTOs review code and understand your architecture, but they shouldn't be your primary developer. If you need someone to write features 30+ hours per week, you need a senior developer, not a fractional CTO.

### Not a Magic Problem-Solver

A fractional CTO can't fix broken processes, unclear requirements, or a toxic culture overnight. They provide guidance and frameworks, but your team still needs to execute.

### Not a Permanent Replacement

Most fractional engagements last 6-18 months, bridging you to a point where you either need a full-time CTO or have grown the internal leadership to manage without one.

## The Deliverables You Should Expect

When you hire a fractional CTO, certain tangible outputs should appear consistently. Use this as a checklist:

### Immediate (First 30 Days)

- **Technical assessment**: Current state analysis of your codebase, infrastructure, and team
- **Risk register**: Documented technical risks with severity ratings and mitigation plans
- **Quick wins identification**: Low-effort, high-impact improvements to tackle first

### Ongoing (Monthly/Quarterly)

- **Technical roadmap**: Prioritised plan linking technical work to business outcomes
- **Team health metrics**: Velocity, quality indicators, and team satisfaction measures
- **Architecture decision records**: Documentation of major technical choices and rationale
- **Budget vs. actual**: Technology spend tracking against plan

### Strategic (Quarterly/Annually)

- **Technical vision**: 12-18 month strategic direction for technology
- **Team growth plan**: Hiring roadmap and organisational design for scaling
- **Technology evaluation**: Assessment of current stack against emerging alternatives
- **Security and compliance review**: Audit results and remediation status

## How Fractional CTOs Spend Their Time

A typical fractional CTO engagement might look like:

| Activity | Time Allocation | Examples |
|----------|-----------------|----------|
| **Strategic planning** | 25% | Roadmap reviews, architecture decisions, board prep |
| **Team leadership** | 25% | 1:1s with engineers, hiring, performance discussions |
| **Operations & process** | 20% | Process improvements, vendor management, security |
| **Hands-on technical work** | 15% | Code review, technical spikes, documentation |
| **Stakeholder communication** | 15% | Leadership meetings, investor updates, requirements clarification |

Note that hands-on coding is typically limited. A fractional CTO who spends most of their time writing code isn't functioning as a CTO—they're functioning as an expensive developer.

## Real-World Examples: Fractional CTO Engagements

To make this concrete, here are three scenarios illustrating what fractional CTO work actually looks like:

### Scenario 1: The Pre-Seed Startup

*Situation*: Two non-technical founders have raised a small pre-seed round. They're working with an offshore dev agency to build their MVP but lack technical oversight.

*Fractional CTO role*:
- Review agency code quality weekly
- Make architecture decisions (database choice, hosting provider)
- Interview and hire the first in-house developer
- Create technical documentation for investor due diligence
- Translate business requirements into clear specs for the agency

*Time commitment*: 2 days per week for 6 months

### Scenario 2: The Post-MVP Scale-Up

*Situation*: A startup with 6 engineers has found product-market fit. The founder who was acting as CTO needs to focus on sales and business development.

*Fractional CTO role*:
- Structure the engineering team into backend and frontend squads
- Implement code review and deployment processes
- Design the hiring plan to grow from 6 to 15 engineers
- Address technical debt that's slowing feature delivery
- Establish security practices for enterprise customers

*Time commitment*: 3 days per week for 12 months

### Scenario 3: The Growth-Stage Preparation

*Situation*: A Series A company with 15 engineers needs to prepare for Series B technical due diligence. Their current tech lead is strong technically but lacks executive experience.

*Fractional CTO role*:
- Audit and document the technical architecture
- Create security and compliance documentation
- Establish engineering metrics and reporting for investors
- Coach the tech lead on leadership and communication
- Represent technology in investor meetings

*Time commitment*: 2 days per week for 4 months, transitioning to advisory

## When You Need a Fractional CTO

Consider hiring a fractional CTO when:

- You have 3-20 engineers but no senior technical leadership
- Technical decisions are becoming complex enough to slow the business
- You're preparing for a funding round and need technical credibility
- Your team is growing and needs structure, process, and mentorship
- You need someone to manage contractors or agencies effectively
- You're navigating a specific technical transition (platform rebuild, security audit)

## Red Flags: When a Fractional CTO Isn't the Answer

Sometimes the problem isn't a lack of CTO-level leadership—it's something else entirely:

- **You need a developer, not a leader**: If you have no engineers and need someone to build the product, hire a developer or agency first
- **Your technical problem is actually a product problem**: Unclear requirements and shifting priorities won't be solved by technical leadership
- **You need immediate, full-time coverage**: Crisis situations often require an interim CTO, not a fractional one
- **You can't commit to the engagement**: Fractional CTOs need access to leadership meetings, team members, and systems to be effective

## Choosing the Right Fractional CTO

Not all fractional CTOs are the same. When evaluating candidates, look for:

**Experience at your stage**: Someone who's scaled from 10 to 100 engineers may not be the best fit for a 3-person team—and vice versa.

**Relevant domain expertise**: B2B SaaS, consumer apps, fintech, and healthcare all have different technical considerations.

**Communication style**: They need to translate technical concepts for non-technical stakeholders effectively.

**Hands-on vs. strategic balance**: Clarify how much coding vs. leadership you need and ensure alignment.

## Conclusion: The Value Proposition

So what does a fractional CTO do exactly? They provide the strategic technical leadership your business needs—without the full-time cost or commitment you don't.

Specifically, they:

- Set technical direction aligned with business goals
- Build and lead engineering teams
- Establish processes that enable scale
- Bridge the gap between technical execution and business strategy
- Deliver tangible deliverables: roadmaps, documentation, team structures, and risk assessments

The best fractional CTOs don't just advise—they become invested in your success. They stick around long enough to see their decisions play out and adjust course based on results.

If you're navigating technical leadership decisions and wondering whether a fractional CTO fits your situation, the next step is defining exactly what problems you need solved and what outcomes you're looking for. That clarity will help you find the right partner—and get maximum value from the engagement.

## Why Startups Choose a Fractional CTO Over Full-Time Hire

Hiring a full-time CTO in New Zealand or Australia typically costs $180,000–$250,000 NZD annually, plus equity, benefits, and recruitment fees. For early-stage startups with 3–15 engineers, that overhead is often unsustainable. A fractional CTO delivers the same strategic leadership at a fraction of the cost—usually $8,000–$15,000 per month—while preserving runway for product development and customer acquisition.

Beyond cost, speed matters. A fractional CTO can start within days, not months. They bring patterns from multiple companies, avoiding the trial-and-error that comes with a first-time CTO learning your industry. They also provide an objective perspective: no internal politics, no legacy baggage, and no hesitation to challenge decisions that aren't serving the business.

## How to Evaluate Fractional CTO Quality

Not every experienced engineer makes a strong fractional CTO. When assessing candidates, look for evidence of these specific capabilities:

- **Track record at your stage**: Ask for examples of companies they've guided from pre-seed to Series A, or from 5 to 20 engineers. The challenges at each stage differ dramatically.
- **Communication clarity**: Can they explain a microservices migration to a non-technical founder in under two minutes? This skill determines whether they'll bridge or widen the business-technology gap.
- **Decision documentation**: Strong fractional CTOs leave written records—architecture decision records, risk registers, roadmap documents—not just verbal recommendations that disappear when they do.
- **Network depth**: The best fractional CTOs can introduce you to vetted developers, reliable vendors, and relevant investors, accelerating your growth beyond their direct hours.

Request a 30-day trial engagement with clear deliverables before committing to a longer contract. This protects both parties and sets expectations for accountability from day one.

## Common Fractional CTO Engagement Structures

Understanding how fractional CTOs structure their time helps you negotiate an arrangement that fits your needs:

| Structure | Time Commitment | Best For | Typical Monthly Cost |
|-----------|-----------------|----------|-------------------|
| **Advisory** | 1–2 days/month | Board guidance, periodic reviews | $3,000–$5,000 NZD |
| **Part-time embedded** | 1–2 days/week | Active roadmap ownership, team leadership | $8,000–$12,000 NZD |
| **Intensive sprint** | 3–4 days/week for 1–3 months | Due diligence prep, crisis turnaround, major transitions | $15,000–$20,000 NZD |
| **Retainer + project** | Variable | Specific deliverables (security audit, architecture review) | Project-based pricing |

Most startups begin with a part-time embedded arrangement and transition to advisory as internal leadership matures. The key is matching the structure to your immediate needs while building toward self-sufficiency.
