# Comprehensive Guide to NZ Startup Grants & Funding 2025
Navigating the startup landscape in New Zealand requires access to funding opportunities and understanding the intricacies of grant applications. This guide offers a thorough overview of 2025's funding landscape, providing insights, tips, and strategies crucial for Kiwi entrepreneurs aiming to secure financial backing and drive their business forward.

In 2026, the funding landscape has shifted significantly. Callaghan Innovation, long the backbone of government R&D support, is being disestablished and its functions transferred to MBIE and other agencies. This change creates both uncertainty and opportunity for founders who understand the new structure. The grants themselves remain fully funded and operational—only the application pathways and supporting agencies have changed.

This guide is designed for Kiwi founders at every stage: pre-revenue startups exploring their first funding options, growing companies scaling R&D programmes, and established businesses seeking commercialisation support. Whether you need $5,000 for business planning or $4 million for market launch, understanding the complete funding ecosystem helps you build a strategic, non-dilutive capital roadmap that preserves founder ownership while accelerating growth.

Whether you're validating your first idea, building an MVP, or scaling an established product, understanding the **startup grants New Zealand 2026** landscape can mean the difference between bootstrapping for years and accelerating your growth with strategic funding. This guide covers every major grant programme available to NZ startups, what's changed in 2026, and how to maximise your chances of approval.

## What's Changed in 2026: The Callaghan Innovation Transition

Before diving into specific grants, it's important to understand the significant structural changes affecting NZ's innovation funding in 2025. Callaghan Innovation, long the cornerstone of government R&D support, is being disestablished with its functions transferring to different agencies.

**Key changes to know:**
- **Funding and grants services** are transferring to MBIE (Ministry of Business, Innovation and Employment)
- **All existing grants remain operational** and fully funded through the transition
- Applications continue to be processed, assessed, and approved as normal
- The Research & Development Tax Incentive, Startup and Founder Support programmes, and all R&D grants continue unchanged

What this means for founders: don't delay applications waiting for clarity. The funding is secure, and MBIE has committed to seamless continuity of support. ## Early-Stage Startup Grants (Pre-Revenue to First Customers)

These programmes support founders from idea validation through to first revenue, typically requiring no existing R&D programme or significant commercial traction.

### Regional Business Partner Capability Vouchers

The Capability Voucher programme is often the first grant New Zealand founders should explore. It's accessible, relatively straightforward, and designed specifically for businesses with fewer than 50 employees.

**What you get:** Up to $5,000 per year in co-funding for training and advisory services

**Eligible uses:**
- Business planning and strategy development
- Financial management and forecasting
- Marketing and customer acquisition planning
- Capital raising preparation
- Governance and leadership training
- Digital systems and process improvements

**Eligibility requirements:**
- Fewer than 50 full-time equivalent employees
- Registered for GST in New Zealand
- Hold a valid New Zealand Business Number (NZBN)
- Operating in a commercial environment
- Demonstrated desire to innovate and grow

**Application tip:** You must complete an assessment with your local Regional Business Partner before applying. Book this early—advisors can help you craft a stronger application and identify which grants to prioritise based on your stage and sector.

### MSD Self-Employment Start-up Payment

For founders transitioning from unemployment or benefits into self-employment, Work and Income offers direct startup funding that doesn't require repayment.

**What you get:** Up to $10,000 for essential startup costs

**Best for:** First-time founders who need modest capital to cover initial expenses like equipment, registration fees, or early marketing

**Important note:** This grant requires you to be on a main benefit (Jobseeker Support, Sole Parent Support, or Supported Living Payment) and genuinely unemployed—not simply leaving employment to start a business. You'll work closely with a case manager to develop a viable business plan before funds are released.

### MSD Flexi-Wage for Self-Employment

This programme provides income support during your first months of trading, helping bridge the gap between starting your business and generating sustainable revenue.

**What you get:** Up to $600 per week for 28 weeks (maximum $16,800)

**How it works:** Unlike a lump-sum grant, Flexi-Wage acts as a wage subsidy, providing regular payments while you establish your business. This can cover personal living expenses or be reinvested into business operations.

### Ārohia Evidence Grant

For startups needing to validate their concept before committing to full development, the Evidence Grant provides co-funding for market research and feasibility studies.

**What you get:** Up to $25,000 in co-funding

**Eligible activities:**
- Market validation and customer research
- Feasibility studies for new products or services
- Ecosystem impact assessments
- Activities that strengthen future grant applications

**Who should apply:** Startups that have outgrown basic validation but aren't yet ready for the larger Trailblazer Grant. If you're planning to apply for significant R&D funding later, using the Evidence Grant to build your case can significantly improve your success rate.

## R&D-Focused Grants (Building Technology and IP)

These programmes require an active R&D programme and are designed for startups developing new technology, products, or processes.

### New to R&D Grant

If your startup hasn't previously received significant government R&D funding, this grant provides substantial support to establish your research capability.

**What you get:** Up to $400,000, covering 40% of eligible R&D activities

**Eligibility requirements:**
- No government R&D funding exceeding $5,000 in the previous three years
- Less than $50,000 total spent on R&D over the previous three years
- Ability to fund your share of R&D costs (60%)
- Genuine technological innovation (not just market innovation)

**Application strategy:** This grant has strict eligibility criteria around previous R&D spending. If you're close to the threshold, consider whether delaying other R&D expenditure could preserve your eligibility. The 40% co-funding rate is generous, but you must demonstrate both technical feasibility and commercial potential.

### R&D Project Grants

For established R&D programmes, Project Grants provide ongoing co-funding for specific research initiatives.

**What you get:** Varies by project scope and stage

**Key considerations:**
- Applications require detailed technical and commercial plans
- Funding is released against milestones
- Regular reporting requirements apply
- IP generated typically remains with your company

### R&D Experience Grant

Hiring technical talent is expensive. The R&D Experience Grant helps startups bring in capable students during university breaks.

**What you get:** Funding for student wages at the Living Wage rate

**How it works:** You employ tertiary students (NZQA levels 6-10) full-time over summer, embedding them in your R&D team. You get motivated, educated extra capacity; students get real-world experience.

**Limits:** Maximum 10 students per application, with ratio requirements based on your existing R&D staff

**Application timing:** Applications for 2026 open in Q3 2025. Students are typically employed over the summer break (November–February). Plan ahead to secure talent and align your R&D milestones.

### R&D Career Grant

For startups ready to bring on graduate-level R&D talent, this grant covers six months of salary for new hires.

**What you get:** 
- Up to $30,000 for Masters graduates
- Up to $35,000 for PhD graduates

**Requirements:**
- Must offer full-time employment (not contract roles)
- Graduate must be in their first R&D role
- Your business must have an active R&D programme with dedicated budget and technical staff
- Pass financial due diligence requirements

**Strategic value:** Beyond the direct funding, this programme helps startups access highly qualified talent that might otherwise be unaffordable. Many successful NZ tech companies trace key early hires back to R&D Career Grant support.

## Growth and Scale Grants (Commercialisation Focus)

These programmes support startups transitioning from development to market, typically requiring demonstrated progress on product and initial market validation.

### Ārohia Trailblazer Grant

The flagship grant for innovative NZ businesses preparing for market launch, the Trailblazer Grant offers substantial co-funding for commercialisation activities.

**What you get:** Up to $4 million in co-funding

**Eligible uses:**
- Infrastructure and production capability setup
- Team building and talent acquisition
- Demonstration model development
- Marketing and market entry activities
- Regulatory compliance and certification

**Eligibility requirements:**
- Majority of R&D completed
- Ready or nearly ready for market launch
- Demonstrated potential to create opportunities for other NZ innovators
- Alignment with four explicit attributes (community impact, industry advancement, etc.)

**Application reality check:** While the $4 million figure attracts attention, successful applicants typically have:
- Significant matched funding committed
- Clear commercialisation pathway
- Strong technical validation
- Credible team with relevant experience

This is not a grant for early validation—it's for companies with proven technology ready to scale. Start with smaller grants to build your case.

### Deep Tech Incubator Programme

For startups working on scientifically complex innovations with long paths to commercialisation, the Deep Tech Incubator provides both funding and structured support.

**What you get:** Minimum $1 million ($750,000 repayable grant from Callaghan Innovation + minimum $250,000 from partner incubator)

**Focus areas:** Scientific breakthroughs, engineering innovations, novel technologies with significant technical risk

**Requirements:**
- Novel, defensible deep tech proposal
- Significant scientific or engineering advancement
- Credible commercialisation strategy
- Early-stage with long development pathway
- Global growth ambition
- Strong science/engineering foundations with initial technical validation

**Strategic note:** The "repayable grant" structure means successful companies eventually return the funding, but without the equity dilution of venture capital. For deep tech startups where development timelines stretch years, this can be significantly more founder-friendly than traditional funding.

## Tax Incentives and Ongoing Support

### R&D Tax Incentive (RDTI)

Unlike grants that provide upfront funding, the R&D Tax Incentive reduces your tax liability based on R&D expenditure, making it valuable for revenue-generating startups and established companies alike.

**What you get:** 15% tax credit on eligible R&D expenditure

**Key features:**
- Available to companies conducting R&D in New Zealand
- No cap on eligible expenditure
- Can be claimed annually
- Complements other grants (though same expenditure cannot be double-counted)

**Eligibility quiz:** Callaghan Innovation provides online quizzes for both business eligibility and R&D eligibility. Complete these before significant R&D investment to ensure your activities qualify.

### R&D Tax Loss Credit

For pre-revenue startups generating tax losses through R&D, this programme allows you to cash out those losses rather than carrying them forward.

**Value:** Receive a refund on R&D-related tax losses before becoming profitable

**Strategic use:** This can provide crucial cash flow for startups in extended development phases, converting accounting losses into actual funding.

## Sector-Specific and Regional Funding

### Ministry for Primary Industries (MPI) Grants

Agritech, food tech, and primary sector startups should explore MPI's funding programmes, which support:
- Sustainable farming initiatives
- Export capability development
- Research and innovation programmes
- Biosecurity protection
- Irrigation and productivity improvements

Funding ranges from small capability grants to significant co-investment for major projects.

### Regional Development Grants

Beyond national programmes, many regions offer specific grants:
- **Christchurch Aerospace Sector Plan:** Dedicated funding for aerospace companies locating in Christchurch
- **Regional Business Partner Network:** Local hubs provide region-specific guidance and sometimes additional funding pools
- **Local Economic Development Agencies:** Many councils offer grants for businesses creating local employment

### Māori Business Development Funding

Dedicated funding pathways support Māori entrepreneurs and Māori-owned businesses, focusing on sustainable enterprise development, capability building, and long-term economic outcomes for Māori communities.

## How to Maximise Your Grant Success Rate

After helping dozens of startups navigate NZ's funding ecosystem, several patterns emerge among successful applicants:

### 1. Start with Your Regional Business Partner

Every founder should begin by booking a free assessment with their local Regional Business Partner. These advisors:
- Understand which grants fit your specific situation
- Can review draft applications before submission
- Often have insight into current funding priorities
- May fast-track applications they've endorsed

The assessment is not a test—it's a genuine support service. Be open about your challenges and funding needs.

### 2. Build a Grant Roadmap, Not Random Applications

Successful startups treat grant funding strategically rather than opportunistically. Map out:
- Which grants you're eligible for now
- Which grants you'll target in 6–12 months
- How each grant builds credibility for the next application
- The total non-dilutive funding potentially available

For example: Capability Voucher → Evidence Grant → New to R&D Grant → Project Grants → Trailblazer Grant. Each successful application strengthens the next.

Choosing the right [tech stack early](/blog/how_to_choose_your_tech_stack_a_non_technical_founders_decision_framework/) also matters—grant assessors often evaluate whether your technical approach is feasible and cost-effective. A well-justified technology choice can strengthen your R&D funding application.

If you're still validating your concept, our guide on [how to validate your startup idea without writing code](/blog/how_to_validate_your_startup_idea_without_writing_a_single_line_of_code/) can help you build the evidence base that makes grant applications compelling.

### 3. Understand the Co-Funding Requirement

Most significant grants require you to contribute 40–60% of project costs. This isn't bureaucratic friction—it ensures you have skin in the game and genuine commitment to outcomes.

**Funding your contribution:**
- Founder investment
- Revenue from existing products
- Smaller grants (stacking is often permitted)
- Angel or seed investment
- Revenue from consulting or services

Document your funding source clearly in applications. Vague commitments to "find the money" significantly reduce approval chances.

### 4. Speak Their Language

Government assessors evaluate against specific criteria. Successful applications directly address:
- **Innovation:** What makes this genuinely new, not just new to your company?
- **Technical risk:** What uncertainties are you resolving through R&D?
- **Commercial potential:** How will this generate economic value for NZ?
- **Capability building:** How does this develop skills, IP, or infrastructure?
- **Ecosystem impact:** How does your success benefit other NZ businesses?

Use the terminology from grant guidelines. If they ask for "ecosystem impact," explicitly address ecosystem impact.

### 5. Demonstrate Progress, Not Just Potential

While early-stage grants accept higher risk, all assessors look for evidence of execution capability:
- Customer conversations conducted
- Prototypes built
- Letters of intent from potential customers
- Team credentials and relevant experience
- Initial market research completed

The gap between "we have an idea" and "we've validated demand" dramatically affects approval likelihood.

### 6. Plan for Reporting Requirements

Grant funding comes with obligations. Before applying, ensure you can:
- Track expenditure against approved categories
- Report progress against milestones
- Maintain appropriate documentation
- Deliver outcomes within agreed timeframes

Nothing harms future applications more than failing to complete reporting on existing grants.

## Common Application Mistakes to Avoid

**Mistake 1: Applying for grants you're not eligible for**
Read eligibility criteria carefully. Some founders waste time on applications that cannot succeed due to business structure, previous funding, or project type.

**Mistake 2: Underestimating the application effort**
Major grants require substantial documentation. Budget 20–40 hours for significant applications, including financial projections, technical specifications, and commercialisation plans.

**Mistake 3: Vague or unrealistic budgets**
Assessors see hundreds of applications. Generic budgets that round to convenient figures suggest insufficient planning. Build detailed, justified cost breakdowns.

**Mistake 4: Ignoring the assessor's perspective**
Remember that assessors are evaluating risk. Address potential concerns proactively—technical risk, market risk, team risk, financial risk.

**Mistake 5: Waiting until you're desperate**
Grant applications take time. Major grants may have 2–3 month assessment periods. Apply before funding becomes critical, not when you're about to run out of runway.

## Conclusion

New Zealand's startup grant ecosystem is among the most supportive globally for early-stage innovation. The combination of non-dilutive funding, regional support networks, and R&D tax incentives creates genuine opportunity for founders willing to navigate the application process.

Success requires strategic thinking: understanding which grants fit your stage, building a credible application that speaks assessor language, and maintaining the documentation discipline that ongoing funding relationships require.

Start with your Regional Business Partner assessment. Explore Capability Vouchers for immediate support. Map your path toward larger grants as you build credibility and traction. And remember—every dollar of grant funding is a dollar less dilution when you do raise equity.

The 2026 changes to Callaghan Innovation don't represent reduced support, just reorganised delivery. The funding is there. The question is whether you'll access it.
