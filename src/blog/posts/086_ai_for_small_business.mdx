## What Does "AI for Small Business" Actually Mean?

When people talk about AI for small business, they usually mean one of three things:

### 1. Generative AI (Creating Content)
AI that writes text, generates images, or produces other content based on your instructions.

**Common uses:**
- Writing emails and proposals
- Creating blog posts and social media content
- Drafting customer responses
- Generating product descriptions

**Example tools:** [ChatGPT](https://openai.com/chatgpt), [Claude](https://claude.ai), Jasper, Copy.ai

### 2. Conversational AI (Customer Interaction)
AI that handles back-and-forth communication with customers or team members.

**Common uses:**
- Website chatbots answering FAQs
- Automated email responses
- Appointment scheduling
- Lead qualification

**Example tools:** [Tidio](https://www.tidio.com), [Intercom](https://www.intercom.com), Drift, custom ChatGPT bots

### 3. Automation AI (Process Efficiency)
AI that connects systems and handles repetitive tasks without human intervention.

**Common uses:**
- Data entry from documents
- Invoice processing
- Meeting transcription and summarisation
- Workflow routing

**Example tools:** [Zapier](https://zapier.com), Make, [Otter.ai](https://otter.ai), Fireflies

Most small businesses should start with generative AI—it's the easiest to implement and delivers immediate time savings. ## Getting Started: Your First 30 Days

### Week 1: Choose Your Starting Point

**Day 1–2: Audit your time**
Track how you spend your working hours for two days. Look for:
- Tasks you do repeatedly (daily or multiple times per week)
- Work that feels like a chore rather than high-value activity
- Processes where consistency matters
- Activities that don't require complex human judgment

**Day 3–4: Select your first AI project**
Choose ONE high-frequency, low-risk task. Good first projects:
- Email writing and responses
- Social media content creation
- Meeting notes and summaries
- Drafting standard documents

**Day 5–7: Set up your first tool**
Sign up for ChatGPT Plus (NZD $30/month) and spend an hour configuring it:
- Add custom instructions about your business
- Create prompt templates for common tasks
- Test with 5–10 real examples

### Week 2: Build the Habit

Use AI daily for your chosen task. Review all output carefully at first—you're learning what works and what doesn't. Refine your prompts based on results. Track time saved.

### Week 3: Measure and Optimise

Calculate actual time savings. Most businesses see 50–70% time reduction on their first AI task. Gather feedback from anyone else using the tool. Document what works in a simple playbook.

### Week 4: Plan Your Expansion

Choose your second AI use case based on what's working. Common next steps:
- Add meeting transcription (Otter.ai)
- Implement customer support chatbot
- Connect tools with Zapier for automation

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake #1: Trying to Automate Everything at Once

**The problem:** Implementing multiple AI tools simultaneously. Team confusion. Poor adoption. No clear ROI.

**The fix:** Start with one tool and one use case. Master it completely before adding complexity. Most businesses get 80% of their AI benefits from their first two tools.

### Mistake #2: Expecting Perfection Immediately

**The problem:** AI output isn't perfect on the first try. Business owners abandon tools before learning to use them effectively.

**The fix:** Treat AI as a draft tool, not a finished product. Expect to edit and refine. The time savings come from starting with good material, not eliminating human review entirely.

### Mistake #3: Ignoring Privacy and Security

**The problem:** Uploading sensitive customer data or confidential business information to public AI tools.

**The fix:** 
- Use business/team plans (ChatGPT Team, not Plus) that don't train on your data
- Remove identifying information before using AI
- Establish clear policies about what can and cannot go into AI tools
- Review tool privacy policies, especially for NZ Privacy Act compliance

### Mistake #4: No Process Documentation

**The problem:** Everyone uses AI differently. Results are inconsistent. Quality varies.

**The fix:** Document your AI workflows. Create shared prompt templates. Establish review standards. Train team members on consistent usage.

### Mistake #5: Choosing Flashy Over Practical

**The problem:** Investing in complex AI solutions when simple tools would deliver more value.

**The fix:** Start with proven, widely-used tools. Solve obvious pain points first. Fancy AI features rarely outperform solid execution of basics.
