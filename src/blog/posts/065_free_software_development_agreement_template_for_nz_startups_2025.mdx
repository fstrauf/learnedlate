# Free Software Development Agreement Template for NZ Startups (2026)

Hiring a developer to build your product is exciting—but without a proper agreement in place, it can quickly turn into a costly nightmare. Who owns the code? What happens if the developer disappears mid-project? How do you handle changes to the scope?

For New Zealand startups, these questions are critical. Unlike larger corporates, you probably don't have a legal team on speed dial. A handshake deal (or worse, a vague email thread) leaves you exposed to intellectual property disputes, budget blowouts, and delayed launches.

This guide walks you through what to include in a software development agreement, why it matters, and provides a **free template** you can use for your next project.

## Why You Need a Software Development Agreement

I've seen too many founders learn this lesson the hard way. A software development agreement isn't just paperwork—it's your insurance policy against the most common issues that derail tech projects.

### Protect Your Intellectual Property

In New Zealand, copyright in software generally belongs to the creator unless there's a written agreement transferring it. Without a contract explicitly assigning IP rights to your company, your developer could technically own the code they wrote for you. This becomes a massive problem when raising investment or selling your business—due diligence will uncover these gaps fast.

### Define Clear Deliverables and Acceptance Criteria

Vague requirements lead to scope creep, missed deadlines, and finger-pointing. A proper agreement defines:

- Exactly what will be delivered (and what won't)
- How you'll test and accept the work
- What happens if something doesn't meet expectations

### Establish Payment Terms and Milestones

Should you pay everything upfront? (Never.) How about monthly retainers versus milestone-based payments? Your agreement should align payments to verifiable progress, protecting your cash flow while keeping the developer motivated.

### Set Expectations for Warranties and Support

What happens if bugs appear three months after launch? Is the developer obligated to fix them? For how long? Clear warranty terms prevent disputes when things inevitably go wrong.

## Key Clauses Every NZ Startup Needs

Whether you're hiring a local Wellington agency, an Auckland freelancer, or an offshore team, your software development agreement template should include these essential clauses.

### 1. Scope of Work and Specifications

This is the heart of your agreement. Be as specific as possible about:

- **Functional requirements**: What features will the software have?
- **Technical specifications**: What technologies will be used? (React, Node.js, AWS, etc.)
- **Deliverables**: Source code, documentation, deployment scripts, design assets
- **Timeline**: Key milestones with specific dates

*Pro tip*: Attach detailed specifications as "Exhibit A" rather than cramming everything into the main agreement. This makes it easier to reference and update if needed.

### 2. Intellectual Property Assignment

This clause should explicitly state that:

- All work product becomes your exclusive property
- The developer assigns all copyright, patents, and other IP rights to your company
- The developer waives any moral rights (important under New Zealand copyright law)
- Any third-party code or open-source components are disclosed and properly licensed

**Critical for NZ startups**: Ensure the agreement specifies that the assignment is unconditional and applies even if the developer terminates the contract early.

### 3. Payment Terms

Structure payments to align incentives:

| Milestone | Payment % | Typical Trigger |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Contract signed | 10-20% | Upon execution |
| Design complete | 20-30% | Design approval |
| Beta/MVP delivered | 30-40% | Acceptance testing complete |
| Final delivery | 20-30% | Production deployment |

Include provisions for:

- Hourly rates for out-of-scope work
- Late payment penalties (if applicable)
- Currency and GST treatment

### 4. Acceptance Testing

Define a clear process for reviewing deliverables:

- **Review period**: How many days do you have to test? (Typically 5-15 business days)
- **Defect notification**: How do you report issues?
- **Correction period**: How long does the developer have to fix problems?
- **Deemed acceptance**: What happens if you don't respond within the review period?

### 5. Confidentiality

Your developer will have access to your business plans, customer data, and trade secrets. A robust confidentiality clause:

- Defines what constitutes confidential information
- Prohibits disclosure to third parties
- Survives termination of the agreement (typically 3-5 years)

For an in-depth look at protecting your business secrets, see our guide on [essential legal agreements for NZ tech startups](TODO: link).

### 6. Warranties and Liability

The developer should warrant that:

- The work will be performed professionally and competently
- The software won't infringe third-party IP rights
- The code will be free from viruses and malware

Limit liability to a reasonable cap (typically the total contract value or 12 months of fees) and exclude liability for consequential damages where permitted by New Zealand law.

### 7. Termination

Include provisions for:

- **Termination for convenience**: Either party can exit with notice (typically 30 days)
- **Termination for cause**: Material breach that isn't fixed within a specified period
- **Effect of termination**: What happens to work-in-progress, payments, and IP rights

### 8. Dispute Resolution

Specify that disputes will first be addressed through good-faith negotiation, then mediation, before escalating to litigation. This saves time and money.

## NZ-Specific Considerations

New Zealand's legal environment has some unique aspects to factor into your agreement.

### Contract and Commercial Law Act 2017

Under the CCLA, certain contract terms may be deemed unfair if they significantly imbalance the parties' rights. Ensure your agreement is commercially reasonable—courts can strike down clauses that heavily favour one party, especially in standard form contracts.

### Privacy Act 2020

If your developer will handle personal information, your agreement must address:

- Compliance with Privacy Act obligations
- Security safeguards
- Notification requirements for privacy breaches
- Return or destruction of personal information upon contract completion

For more on data protection obligations, read our article on [privacy compliance for NZ startups](TODO: link).

### GST and Tax Implications

Clearly state whether quoted prices include or exclude GST. If you're engaging an offshore developer, consider withholding tax obligations and whether they need to register for NZ GST.

### Employment vs. Contractor Status

Be careful not to inadvertently create an employment relationship. The agreement should clearly state that the developer is an independent contractor, not an employee. Key indicators include:

- The developer controls how and when work is done
- They can engage subcontractors
- They bear their own business risks and expenses

## Red Flags to Watch For

Before signing any development agreement, watch out for these warning signs:

**🚩 The developer refuses to assign IP rights**

Some agencies try to retain ownership of the code, granting you only a license. This is a dealbreaker if you plan to raise investment or sell your company.

**🚩 No clear acceptance criteria**

Vague language like "deliver a working application" without defining "working" leads to disputes.

**🚩 Payment required upfront**

Never pay 100% upfront. A reasonable deposit (10-20%) is standard, but the bulk should be tied to deliverables.

**🚩 No warranty period**

A reputable developer will stand behind their work with at least a 30-90 day warranty period to fix bugs.

**🚩 Unclear change request process**

Projects evolve. Your agreement needs a clear process for handling scope changes—including how additional costs will be calculated and approved.

## When to Get Legal Advice

While this template covers the essentials, there are situations where you should definitely engage a lawyer:

- **Complex projects** involving multiple developers, significant third-party integrations, or high technical risk
- **Investment preparation** where investors will scrutinise IP ownership
- **Offshore development** with developers in jurisdictions with different IP laws
- **Licensing arrangements** where you're building on existing developer IP

New Zealand has several good options for startup-friendly legal advice:

- **Community Law Centres** offer free initial advice
- **Innovation hubs** like Creative HQ or Icehouse often have legal clinic sessions
- **Fixed-fee packages** from firms specialising in startups (expect $500-$2,000 for contract review)

## Free Software Development Agreement Template

Below is a template you can adapt for your project. **Important**: This is a starting point, not legal advice. Have it reviewed by a lawyer before using it for significant projects.

---

### SOFTWARE DEVELOPMENT AGREEMENT

**Date:** [DATE]

**Between:**

**[DEVELOPER NAME]**

Address: [DEVELOPER ADDRESS]

("Developer")

**And:**

**[YOUR COMPANY NAME]**

Company Number: [NZBN]

Address: [YOUR ADDRESS]

("Client")

---

#### 1. SERVICES

The Developer agrees to develop the software described in **Exhibit A** (the "Software") in accordance with the specifications, milestones, and timeline set out in **Exhibit B**.

#### 2. COMPENSATION

The Client shall pay the Developer:

- **Total Fee:** $[AMOUNT] [excluding/including] GST
- **Payment Schedule:** As set out in Exhibit B
- **Additional Work:** $[RATE] per hour, subject to prior written approval

Invoices are payable within [14/30] days of receipt.

#### 3. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

3.1 **Assignment:** Upon full payment, the Developer assigns to the Client all right, title, and interest in the Software, including source code, documentation, and all intellectual property rights.

3.2 **Pre-existing Materials:** The Developer retains rights to any pre-existing code, tools, or frameworks incorporated into the Software, granting the Client a perpetual, royalty-free license to use them as part of the Software.

3.3 **Third-Party Code:** The Developer must disclose and obtain appropriate licenses for any open-source or third-party code used.

3.4 **Moral Rights:** The Developer waives all moral rights in the Software to the extent permitted by law.

#### 4. ACCEPTANCE

4.1 **Review Period:** The Client has [10] business days from delivery to test the Software.

4.2 **Defects:** The Client may notify the Developer of any non-conformity with the specifications. The Developer will correct defects within [10] business days.

4.3 **Deemed Acceptance:** If the Client does not notify defects within the review period, the Software is deemed accepted.

#### 5. WARRANTIES

The Developer warrants that for [90] days from acceptance:

- The Software will conform substantially to the specifications
- The Software will be free from material defects
- The Software will not contain viruses or malicious code
- The Software does not infringe third-party intellectual property rights

#### 6. CONFIDENTIALITY

Each party agrees to:

- Keep confidential information strictly confidential
- Use confidential information only for purposes of this Agreement
- Not disclose confidential information to third parties without consent

This obligation survives termination for [3] years.

#### 7. TERM AND TERMINATION

7.1 **Term:** This Agreement commences on [DATE] and continues until completion or termination.

7.2 **Termination for Convenience:** Either party may terminate with [30] days' written notice.

7.3 **Termination for Cause:** Either party may terminate immediately if the other party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within [14] days of notice.

7.4 **Effect of Termination:** Upon termination, the Developer must deliver all work-in-progress and the Client must pay for all completed work.

#### 8. LIABILITY

8.1 **Limitation:** Each party's total liability under this Agreement is limited to the Total Fee paid or payable.

8.2 **Exclusion:** Neither party is liable for indirect, consequential, or lost profits.

#### 9. GENERAL

9.1 **Governing Law:** This Agreement is governed by the laws of New Zealand.

9.2 **Disputes:** Parties will first attempt to resolve disputes through good-faith negotiation, then mediation.

9.3 **Entire Agreement:** This Agreement (including Exhibits) constitutes the entire agreement between the parties.

9.4 **Amendment:** Amendments must be in writing signed by both parties.
