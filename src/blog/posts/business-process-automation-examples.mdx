## What Counts as Business Process Automation?

Before we dive into examples, let's ground ourselves. Business process automation means using software to handle repetitive tasks that humans currently do manually. For a deeper plain-English explanation, see [what is business process automation](/blog/what-is-business-process-automation/).

It's not about replacing people—it's about freeing them from tedious work so they can focus on tasks that actually move the needle.

**Three types of automation you'll see in this article:**

1. **Task automation**: Single repetitive actions (data entry, email responses)
2. **Workflow automation**: Multi-step processes (customer onboarding, invoice processing)
3. **Decision automation**: Rule-based decisions (lead scoring, inventory reordering)

Now let's look at how real businesses implement these.

## Sales & Marketing Automation Examples

### 1. Lead Qualification and Routing

**The Manual Process:**
Sarah's Auckland-based consulting firm received 50+ enquiries per week. Her team manually reviewed each form submission, researched the company, assigned a score, and forwarded qualified leads to sales reps. Average time: 15 minutes per lead.

**The Automated Solution:**
- Web form captures lead data
- Zapier enriches contact info via Clearbit
- Automated scoring based on company size, industry, budget
- High-quality leads automatically create Salesforce opportunities and notify reps via Slack
- Low-quality leads enter nurture sequence

**Results:**
- Lead processing time: 15 minutes → 30 seconds
- Response time to hot leads: 4 hours → 8 minutes
- Conversion rate: +23% (faster response = higher close rate)
- Cost: NZD $150/month in tools
- Time saved: 12.5 hours/week

### 2. Social Media Content Distribution

**The Manual Process:**
A Christchurch e-commerce brand manually posted to Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, and LinkedIn. Content lived in scattered Google Docs. The marketing coordinator spent 6 hours weekly copying, pasting, resizing images, and scheduling posts.

**The Automated Solution:**
- Content calendar in Notion
- Buffer automatically pulls posts from Notion via API
- Images auto-resize for each platform
- Posts schedule based on engagement data
- Performance metrics flow back into Notion

**Results:**
- Content distribution time: 6 hours → 45 minutes weekly
- Posting consistency: 3x/week → 5x/week (no extra work)
- Engagement rate: +41% (optimal timing automation)
- Cost: NZD $65/month
- ROI: 8:1 in first quarter

### 3. Email Nurture Sequences

**The Manual Process:**
A Wellington SaaS startup sent welcome emails manually. New signups waited hours (or days) for human follow-up. No systematic education sequence existed.

**The Automated Solution:**
- New signup triggers welcome email sequence
- 7-email drip over 14 days
- Content tailored by user role and company size
- Engagement tracked; hot users flagged for sales outreach
- Abandoned trial users receive targeted retention emails

**Results:**
- Time to first email: 4+ hours → instant
- Trial-to-paid conversion: 8% → 14%
- Support tickets: -30% (better onboarding education)
- Implementation time: 3 days
- Annual revenue impact: +NZD $127K

## Operations & Admin Automation Examples

### 4. Invoice Processing and Payment

**The Manual Process:**
A 12-person design agency received 40+ supplier invoices monthly via email. Someone manually downloaded PDFs, entered data into [Xero](/blog/free_accounting_software_nz_guide/), matched to purchase orders, and routed for approval. Average processing time: 20 minutes per invoice.

**The Automated Solution:**
- Invoices forwarded to dedicated email address
- Optical Character Recognition (OCR) extracts data
- System matches invoice to PO automatically
- Approval workflow routes to correct manager
- Approved invoices auto-post to Xero
- Payment scheduled based on terms

**Results:**
- Processing time per invoice: 20 minutes → 2 minutes
- Data entry errors: 12% → 1%
- Early payment discount capture: +NZD $8,400/year
- Implementation cost: NZD $1,200 setup + $80/month
- Payback period: 4 months

### 5. Employee Onboarding

HR at a 50-person tech company spent 8 hours per new hire coordinating equipment, accounts, training, and paperwork. Checklist lived in Excel. Follow-ups happened via scattered emails.

**The Automated Solution:**

- Workflow automatically:
  - Creates email account and workspace access
  - Orders laptop and equipment
  - Schedules training sessions
  - Assigns onboarding buddy
  - Sends day 1, week 1, month 1 check-in surveys
  - Tracks completion and alerts HR of blockers

**Results:**
- HR time per new hire: 8 hours → 1.5 hours
- Onboarding completion rate: 73% → 96%
- New hire satisfaction: +2.1 points (out of 5)
- Equipment delays: -89%
- Cost: NZD $200/month

### 6. Meeting Scheduling and Preparation

## How to Choose Your First Automation Project

You've seen what's possible. Now, where should *you* start?

**Use this framework:**

### 1. Map Your Repetitive Processes

Spend one week tracking tasks that:
- Happen more than once per week
- Follow the same steps every time
- Don't require complex human judgment
- Cause bottlenecks or frustration

### 2. Calculate the "Automation Score"

For each process, score 1-10 on:
- **Frequency**: How often it happens
- **Time cost**: Hours spent weekly
- **Error rate**: How often mistakes occur
- **Ease**: How straightforward to automate

Multiply these together. Highest score = your first target.

### 3. Estimate ROI

**Time saved annually** (hours) × **hourly cost** (salary + overhead) = **annual benefit**

Compare to:
- Tool costs (monthly × 12)
- Setup costs (consulting, implementation time)
- Training time

If payback period is under 12 months, it's probably worth it.

### 4. Start Small

Don't automate your entire business at once. Pick one process. Get it working. Learn. Then expand.

## Common Automation Mistakes to Avoid

**Don't automate broken processes.** Fix the process first, then automate it. Automation amplifies—if your process is inefficient, you'll just do inefficient things faster.

**Don't over-engineer.** The best automation is often the simplest. A Zapier workflow connecting two tools beats a custom-coded solution that takes 3 months to build.

**Don't forget the humans.** Some processes *should* stay manual. Customer conversations that build relationships, creative strategy work, complex problem-solving—these aren't automation candidates.

**Don't set and forget.** Automation needs maintenance. Tools change, processes evolve, edge cases appear. Review your automations quarterly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the easiest business process to automate first?**
Start with a high-frequency, rule-based task you do daily — like lead capture to CRM entry, invoice reminders, or social media scheduling. Processes that take 15–30 minutes and happen 5+ times per week give the fastest visible ROI.

**How much does business process automation cost for small business?**
Most NZ small businesses start with tools costing NZD $50–300/month (Zapier, Make, or n8n). The 16 examples in this article average NZD $150/month in tool costs with ROI of 6–12x in the first year.

**What tools do NZ businesses use for automation?**
The most popular tools are Zapier (easiest to start), Make/Integromat (more powerful), Xero for accounting automation, and ActiveCampaign or HubSpot for sales/marketing sequences.

**How long does it take to automate a business process?**
Simple automations (single trigger → single action) take 1–2 hours to set up. Multi-step workflows with conditions and error handling typically take a day. The examples in this guide were all built in under a week.
