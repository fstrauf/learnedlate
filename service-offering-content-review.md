# Service Offering Content Review

## Scope

This review covers the active AI service offering shown in site navigation and primary service routes:

- Home: `src/views/HomePage.vue`
- Services hub: `src/views/ServicesPage.vue`
- AI Readiness: `src/views/AiReadinessPage.vue`
- Agentic Workflows: `src/views/AgenticWorkflowsPage.vue`
- Custom AI Development: `src/views/CustomAiDevelopmentPage.vue`
- Full-Stack Delivery: `src/views/FullStackDeliveryPage.vue`

It does **not** focus on legacy SEO landing pages that are still routed but removed from nav.

## Executive Summary

The current service offering feels overcomplicated for three main reasons:

1. The offer structure mixes different kinds of things.
2. The site explains too much before stating clearly what you sell.
3. The same ideas repeat across multiple sections and pages.

The result is that a visitor has to work too hard to answer four simple questions:

1. What do you do?
2. Who is it for?
3. What should I buy first?
4. Why are these services different from each other?

Right now the site has strong ideas, but they are spread across too many layers: service categories, delivery model, process model, audience tiers, and positioning language.

## Core Findings

### 1. The offer hierarchy is unclear

The four primary services are not the same type of offer:

- **AI Readiness Assessment** is an entry diagnostic.
- **Agentic Workflows** is a solution category.
- **Custom AI Development** is a special-case build path.
- **Full-Stack Delivery** is a delivery model.

Because they are presented as four parallel services, the offer feels more complex than it is.

### 2. The homepage takes too long to reach the actual offer

The homepage leads with positioning, then problem framing, then methodology, then audience segmentation, and only later gets to the service list.

That means the user reads a lot of context before they get a clean answer to: **what can I hire you for?**

### 3. The same strategic promise appears too many times

These themes repeat across the homepage and service pages:

- strategy to execution
- no handoff gaps
- accountability
- measurable outcomes
- practical implementation

Those are good ideas, but they are being restated rather than sharpened.

### 4. The copy uses too much insider language

Examples:

- "vendor theater"
- "pilot purgatory"
- "agentic"
- "competitive differentiation through custom AI capabilities"

These phrases sound sharp, but they increase interpretation cost. The offer should feel easier to buy, not more intellectually dense.

### 5. The services page has too many decision frameworks on one page

It currently contains:

- service cards
- a five-phase journey
- engagement models
- audience tiers

Each of those is reasonable on its own. Together they create too many axes of choice.

### 6. Some pages explain method before value

- The Agentic Workflows page starts by defining agentic AI.
- The Custom AI page moves quickly into technical capability lists.
- The Full-Stack page expands deeply into process and governance.

For most buyers, the first priority is not understanding your framework. It is understanding whether you can solve their problem.

### 7. The broader public site still dilutes the main offer

Even though older pages are removed from nav, the public route set still includes multiple unrelated or semi-related offers.

That may be acceptable for SEO, but it weakens the clarity of the overall brand if those pages remain discoverable.

## Recommended Offer Structure

The site should communicate:

- one core promise
- one clear starting point
- three main offers

### Recommended Core Promise

**LearnedLate helps ANZ mid-market teams identify, build, and deploy one high-impact AI workflow.**

This is clearer than stacking multiple abstractions like strategy, transformation, agentic systems, and full-stack delivery all at once.

### Recommended Service Ladder

#### 1. AI Readiness Assessment

For teams that know AI matters but do not know where to start.

What it does:

- clarifies readiness
- identifies the best first use cases
- gives a practical next step

#### 2. AI Workflow Automation

For teams with a clear process they want mapped, automated, integrated, and piloted.

What it does:

- targets a specific workflow
- integrates with real systems
- moves from prototype to pilot to deployment

#### 3. Delivery Partnership

For larger or more complex programs that need the same team from discovery through rollout.

What it does:

- keeps context in one team
- manages sequencing and implementation
- stays accountable for operational outcomes

### Where Custom AI Fits

Custom AI should probably not be a first-order offer unless it is a major standalone revenue line.

A simpler framing is:

> We use custom development only when standard tools are not enough.

That makes custom AI a capability inside the main offers, not a parallel offer that the buyer has to evaluate separately.

## What To Cut or Compress

### Remove or compress repeated positioning claims

Reduce repeated references to:

- strategy to execution
- accountability
- no handoff gaps
- measurable outcomes
- practical AI

Pick one primary line and let the rest support it indirectly.

### Reduce category explanation

Do less explaining of:

- what agentic AI is
- why custom development exists in theory
- internal delivery philosophy

Do more showing of:

- the kind of business problem you solve
- what the client gets
- what happens next

### Reduce decision surfaces

On the main services page, a buyer should not have to process all of the following at once:

- service type
- audience size
- engagement model
- lifecycle stage

Choose one primary organizing system. The best candidate is likely the **service ladder**.

## Copy Principles To Use In Rewrite

### 1. Lead with the buyer problem, not the framework

Prefer:

- We help you identify the best first AI workflow to automate.

Over:

- We provide end-to-end strategy-to-execution transformation.

### 2. Prefer plain language over category language

Prefer:

- AI workflow automation

Over:

- agentic workflow implementation

### 3. Reduce abstraction

Prefer:

- Automate one high-friction workflow in 90 days.

Over:

- Build autonomous systems that handle complete business processes end-to-end.

### 4. Make each page answer one buying question

- Homepage: What do you do and where do I start?
- Services page: Which option fits me?
- AI Readiness page: What do I get and should I start here?
- Workflow Automation page: Can you automate a real process for us?
- Delivery Partnership page: Can you own the whole program?

### 5. Cut any sentence that only restates positioning

If a sentence does not clarify:

- audience
- outcome
- deliverable
- next step

it is a candidate for removal.

## Practical Rewrite Plan

### Phase 1: Simplify the offer architecture

- Decide whether the core structure is 3 offers or 4 offers.
- Decide whether Custom AI remains in nav.
- Decide whether Full-Stack Delivery remains a standalone service or becomes a delivery model.

### Phase 2: Rewrite the homepage around the simplified offer
